The Question

What if color had a sound? And what if that sound could be translated back into something visual — but transformed by the journey?

Visual Reverb began with a deeply personal observation: growing up with ADHD shaped a unique sensory perception where color wasn't just seen — it was experienced as frequency. This project set out to make that invisible connection visible, measurable, and shareable.

The complete installation — 9 color inputs (top) and their frequency-transformed visual outputs (bottom)

The Process

01

Scientific Analysis

Each of 9 colors was analyzed using spectrophotometry — measuring precise wavelength values (380–700nm range) to extract the actual frequency signature of each hue.

02

Algorithmic Translation

Frequency data was mapped algorithmically to sound parameters — pitch, timbre, resonance. The color's scientific identity became its musical voice.

03

Visual Synthesis

The resulting audio was run through Chladni plate visualizations and cymatics — sound made visible. These patterns became the source for the second set of paintings.

04

The Album

Every track on the full instrumental album corresponds to one of the nine colors. Listening is experiencing the spectrum — from violet to red — as a complete sonic journey.

The Outcome

Visual Reverb was exhibited at a Santa Barbara gallery and later extended to MOXI — The Wolf Museum of Exploration + Innovation — where it engaged approximately 1,500 visitors per day.

The project demonstrated a complete multidisciplinary capability: scientific methodology, fine art execution, music production, environmental design, and exhibition curation — unified by a single conceptual thread.
