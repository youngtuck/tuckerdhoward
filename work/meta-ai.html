The Scale

Industrial-volume visual production. What 300 assets per day actually means.

At Meta, the volume of AI-generated visual content isn't measured in projects — it's measured in daily throughput. As an AI Model Designer on the Human Behavior and Ads Creative team, the work was continuous, high-stakes, and unforgiving: approximately 300 assets reviewed and refined every day, each one destined for global advertising campaigns reaching millions of people.

Scale in context

Chapter 02

The Prompt System

Structured prompt engineering that improved alignment, lighting, and brand integrity across the pipeline.

The deeper contribution wasn't reviewing assets — it was building the systems that made better assets possible in the first place. Structured prompt frameworks were developed to improve AI output quality before it reached the evaluation stage, targeting the specific failure modes that appeared repeatedly at volume: misaligned lighting, typography collisions, brand drift, and cultural inconsistency.

The highest-leverage move in AI production isn't better review — it's better input architecture. Fix the prompt system and you fix thousands of downstream outputs simultaneously.

Prompt engineering philosophy · Meta Ads Creative
Structured prompt architecture — illustrative framework Conceptual only · Not Meta IP
Subject

Core asset definition. Product, person, or environment. Specificity here reduces ambiguity throughout the entire generation chain.

Lighting

Light source, direction, and quality. The single highest-impact parameter for photorealism. Vague lighting instructions produce incoherent shadows and incorrect material response.

Brand

Platform-specific identity constraints. Color palette, typography safe zones, visual tone. Applied as hard constraints, not suggestions.

Composition

Spatial relationships and hierarchy. Where the eye goes first, second, third — encoded into the prompt, not left to the model's default behavior.

Negative

Explicit exclusions. Known AI failure modes for this asset type. Prevents the model from solving the wrong problem elegantly.

Actual prompt systems developed at Meta are confidential. This framework is illustrative of general methodology only.

Chapter 03

The Craft

Visual quality assurance at a level that protects brand integrity globally — with the judgment to know the difference between a flaw and a choice.

AI-generated imagery fails in specific, learnable ways. The job of a visual QA designer at this level isn't just spotting problems — it's building a taxonomy of failure modes, understanding their causes, and knowing which ones matter at scale. Not every artifact is equal. Some are invisible at delivery size. Some become critical at certain aspect ratios. Knowing the difference is the craft.

The QA distinction

Automated systems catch technical failures. Human visual judgment catches brand failures — the asset that's technically correct but feels wrong. That gap is where the work lives.

Visual QA philosophy Human Behavior & Ads Creative · Meta

Cross-functional collaboration was a core part of the role — partnering with teams to optimize AI-integrated production workflows, not just flagging problems but improving the systems that created them. At 300 assets per day, small workflow improvements have outsized downstream impact.
